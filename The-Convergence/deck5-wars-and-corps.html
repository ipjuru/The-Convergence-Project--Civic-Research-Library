Chapter 6 — Private Equity

When PE Buys Your Hospital, People Die

Private equity manages $3.1 trillion in U.S. assets and has bought its way into hospitals, nursing homes, emergency rooms, veterinary clinics, and housing. The data on outcomes is not good.

Nursing homes (NBER study): PE acquisition led to a 10% increase in mortality — approximately 20,150 additional deaths over 12 years. Antipsychotic drug use rose 50%. Billing increased 19% per stay. Staffing hours decreased. Harvard Medical School found PE hospital acquisitions caused 7 additional deaths per 10,000 ER visits. Steward Health Care — PE-owned — went bankrupt, eliminating one Florida community's only emergency room.

Source: NBER · Harvard Medical School · Chicago Booth Review · Sen. Warren PE report
