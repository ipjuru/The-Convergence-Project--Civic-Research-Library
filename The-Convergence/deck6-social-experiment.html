Chapter 1

The Scale of What We're Living In

Social media isn't a tool people use. For billions of people it has become the primary lens through which they experience reality, news, relationships, and self-worth. The data on time and access is staggering.

Chapter 2 — The Research

What It's Doing to Mental Health

182 peer-reviewed studies covering over 1.1 million subjects. A U.S. Surgeon General warning. Two rounds of Congressional testimony. The signal is clear even if the causal debate continues.

Facebook's own internal research (leaked by Frances Haugen): 32% of teen girls who felt bad about their bodies said Instagram made it worse. 17% said eating disorders worsened on Instagram. 13.5% of UK teen girls said Instagram intensified suicidal thoughts. Internal slides said: "We make body image issues worse for one in three teen girls." This was known. They chose not to fix it.

Source: Journal of Affective Disorders 2024 (n=1,169,396) · US Surgeon General 2023 · Facebook internal leaks via Frances Haugen

Chapter 3 — The Hardest Data

Youth Suicide and Social Media

Correlation is not causation — and researchers are still fighting over the causal link. But the numbers on the ground are not abstract. Children are in crisis at measurable rates, and the timeline matches.

March 2026 — Landmark ruling: A jury found Meta liable for child sexual exploitation. A separate California trial found Meta and YouTube liable for intentionally designing addictive platforms that injured a young woman's mental health. Source: CDC Youth Risk Behavior Survey · ACHI · Social Media Victims Law Center · CNN March 2026 ruling

Chapter 4 — The Design Was Intentional

The Algorithm Is Optimized for Outrage

This is not a side effect. Internal documents from Facebook, Instagram, and YouTube reveal that platforms knew their algorithms amplified harmful content — and chose engagement over wellbeing.

Frances Haugen 2021

Facebook Knew

Internal research confirmed "content that is hateful, divisive, and polarizing" drives the most engagement. Facebook dissolved its Civic Integrity team after the 2020 election. The algorithm was not an accident — it was the product.

Doom Scroll Science

Variable Ratio Reinforcement

Each scroll releases dopamine via the same mechanism as slot machines. Continuous distressing content activates cortisol and adrenaline, keeping the amygdala hyperactivated. Blue light suppresses melatonin. The architecture of the feed is neurologically hostile.

Chapter 5 — The Scam Economy

$16.6 Billion Stolen Online in One Year

The FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center reported $16.6 billion in losses in 2024 — a 33% increase from the prior year. The FTC estimates actual losses could be as high as $195.9 billion when unreported fraud is included.

Who gets hit hardest: Adults over 60 lost ~$4.8 billion in 2024 — an 83% increase year-over-year. Pig butchering scams alone saw $75 billion in crypto flows tracked to scammer addresses (2020-2024). The UN estimates 200,000+ people are held in scam compounds in Southeast Asia as trafficking victims. AI now allows voice cloning from 3 seconds of audio with 85% accuracy — deepfake fraud losses projected at $40 billion by 2027.

Source: FBI IC3 2024 · FTC 2025 · McKnight's Senior Living · UT Dallas study · Keepnet Labs 2026

Chapter 6 — The Reality Crisis

Deepfakes: 550% Surge in Two Years

AI-generated content has moved from novelty to weapon. The scale of deepfake production — and the harm categories it's driving — is accelerating faster than any regulatory response.

The most disturbing surge: AI-generated child sexual abuse material reported to NCMEC went from 4,700 (2023) to 67,000 (2024) — a 1,325% increase. AI-generated CSAM videos went from 13 (2024) to 3,443 (2025) — a 26,385% increase. 96-98% of all deepfake content is pornographic and overwhelmingly targets women. Political deepfakes were documented in 38 countries during elections between 2023-2024. Only 0.1% of participants in a 2025 study correctly identified all fake and real media.

Source: Keepnet Labs 2026 · UNESCO · NCMEC / Childlight · IWF 2026 · iProov 2025

Chapter 7 — AI at Work

The Layoff Wave Nobody Is Being Straight About

Companies are citing AI in layoffs at an accelerating rate. The honest version: many layoffs attributed to AI are actually about cost-cutting dressed up in tech language — but the displacement is real either way.

The CFO survey data (Duke, 2025): 44% of CFOs plan AI-related cuts, with an estimated ~502,000 roles expected lost in 2026. Jack Dorsey (Block) cut 4,000 jobs (40%) of workforce in February 2026: "Intelligence tools have changed what it means to build and run a company." Amazon cut 30,000+ corporate roles since 2023. Media lost 3,434+ journalism jobs in 2025. Critical caveat: Harvard Business Review found companies are "laying off because of AI's potential — not its performance." New York State found zero of 160 companies checked the AI box on layoff disclosure forms.

Source: Challenger Gray · Duke CFO Survey · Fortune March 2026 · Press Gazette · Harvard Business Review · CBS News 2026

Chapter 8 — The Paradox

The Most Connected Generation Is the Loneliest

The Surgeon General declared loneliness a national epidemic. Young people have more "connections" than any generation in history and less genuine human contact. The body is paying for it.

The good news: People are waking up. 56% of people consider themselves addicted to their devices. 44% of Gen Z intentionally reduced screen time in the past 6 months. Vinyl records have seen 17 consecutive years of growth. Live Nation reported its biggest concert attendance year ever in 2025. Running clubs, book clubs, and community organizations are surging. Gen Z is driving what researchers are calling a Social Renaissance — people want real connection again and are actively seeking it.

Source: US Surgeon General 2023 · CivicScience · Fortune Feb 2026 · Reach Out Recovery 2026 · Tunheim research

Chapter 9

A Balanced Path Through the Digital Age

AI integration may be here to stay. The question is whether we steer it or get steered. Here's what protecting yourself and your community actually looks like.

For You

Protect Your Mind

  • Set screen time limits — the research is unambiguous that reducing social media use reduces depression symptoms
  • Doom scrolling activates your threat response — your brain cannot tell news from predator. Protect your nervous system.
  • Phone-free meals and first/last hours of day are measurably protective
  • Know the difference between a parasocial relationship and a real one

For Kids

Set the Rules Early

  • Australia banned under-16s from social media (December 2025) — 31 U.S. states now require school phone bans
  • No phones in bedrooms. Blue light at night suppresses melatonin — this is physiology, not preference.
  • The algorithm shows children extreme content to maximize engagement. Supervise and discuss.
  • Child influencer labor laws barely exist — know what your kids are consuming and creating

Big Picture

Demand Accountability

  • Support the Kids Online Safety Act and data privacy legislation
  • Demand warning labels on social media platforms — the Surgeon General called for them
  • Support the EU AI Act model for transparency and algorithmic accountability
  • Join the analog renaissance — vinyl, live music, in-person community, real conversation
