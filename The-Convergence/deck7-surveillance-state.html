Chapter 1 — The Foundation

They Built This Long Before Trump

The surveillance state didn't start in 2025. It was built post-9/11, normalized under Obama, and is now being weaponized with unprecedented scope. Understanding the architecture requires knowing what was already there.

Oct 2001
PATRIOT Act signed 357-66 in the House, 98-1 in the Senate. Authorized roving wiretaps, "sneak and peek" searches, compelled production of "any tangible thing" without probable cause, National Security Letters without judicial oversight.
2007–2013
PRISM launched — collected emails, video chats, photos, social data from Microsoft, Yahoo, Google, Facebook, Apple. Revealed by Snowden in 2013. Described as "the number one source of raw intelligence" for NSA reports. Never shut down.
2024
Section 702 of FISA — legal backbone of warrantless foreign intelligence collection — reauthorized for only 2 years, shortest extension ever. A December 2024 federal court ruling found warrantless U.S. person queries violate the Fourth Amendment. Expires April 20, 2026.
March 2025
Trump reverses course, backs Section 702 reauthorization — after previously posting "KILL FISA." The surveillance infrastructure is now bipartisan property regardless of who's in charge of it.

Source: ACLU · EPIC · Congress.gov R48592 · Reason Magazine March 2026 · Brookings

Chapter 2 — DOGE

"The Largest Data Breach in American History"

Elon Musk's DOGE team accessed the personal data of hundreds of millions of Americans across federal agencies — with young staffers who had minimal vetting and maximum access.

Treasury

Tax refunds, bank accounts, Social Security payments for all recipients

SSA

SSNs, medical records, immigration records for every American

OPM / HHS / Ed

Federal employee records, student loan data, patient records

The Supreme Court weighed in: June 6, 2025 — SCOTUS (6-3) granted DOGE unlimited access to SSA data, staying lower court injunctions. Justice Jackson's dissent: "This is a sad day for our democracy and a scary day for millions of people." DOGE was also shielded from FOIA requests. Connecticut AG Tong called it "the largest data breach in American history." Acting SSA Commissioner Michelle King resigned rather than provide access.

Source: ScheerPost · Freedom House · IBTimes · EFF · SCOTUS June 2025

Chapter 3 — Palantir

The $300 Billion Company That Knows Everything

Founded with CIA seed money. Now valued at ~$300 billion. Holds a $10 billion Army contract. Powers ICE deportations. The CEO says their product is "used, on occasion, to kill people." And they're just getting started.

Peter Thiel's fingerprints: Net worth $23.9 billion. Primary financial backer of VP JD Vance. Multiple Thiel-network figures hold administration positions. Stephen Miller holds a financial stake in Palantir. Palantir CEO Alex Karp's book "The Technological Republic" calls for a state that "looks more like a startup" led by a "founder-like figure." EFF analyst: "Immigrants are the first target — it is a permission structure. If they can get away with it on immigrants, what are the legal barriers from moving to the next undesirable group?"

Source: State of Surveillance · BBC Science Focus · Freedom House · ScheerPost · IBTimes

Chapter 4 — The Industry Nobody Regulates

$300 Billion Data Broker Industry. Zero Federal Law.

Up to 5,000 data brokers operate globally, collecting thousands of data points on every American — and selling them to the highest bidder, including the government, without a warrant.

The warrant loophole: Government agencies purchase personal data from brokers to bypass Fourth Amendment protections. The FBI, DHS, ICE, DEA, IRS, and local police are documented purchasers. The DOD purchased location data from prayer apps to monitor Muslim communities. Police purchased data to track racial justice protesters. FBI Director Kash Patel (March 2026) declined to commit to not buying Americans' location data. 80% of Americans say agencies should need a warrant. There is no federal law requiring it.

Source: NPR/Houston Public Media March 2026 · EFF · Brookings · Freedom House · FTC

Chapter 5 — The Real Power Structure

Who Actually Rules America

When 300 billionaires spend $3 billion on a single election cycle, when the top 1% holds 31% of all wealth, when lobbyists outnumber senators 10 to 1 — elections are one of several mechanisms. Not the only one.

The billionaire-to-government pipeline is direct: At least 12 billionaires hold roles in the Trump second-term administration, worth a collective $390-460+ billion. Donor-appointees gave $52+ million to Trump or pro-Trump PACs before their appointments. Musk holds 100+ federal contracts across 17 agencies while heading DOGE. His 2024 political contributions were 4× his average annual federal taxes (2013-2018). $4.44 billion was spent on lobbying in 2024 — a record. Nearly two-thirds of former Congress members now become lobbyists.

Source: Forbes · OpenSecrets · Brookings · Center for American Progress · EPI

Chapter 6 — The Myth vs. The Data

The American Dream Has Statistically Halved

Raj Chetty's landmark research is clear: the probability of earning more than your parents has dropped from 92% (born 1940) to roughly 50% (born 1984). This is not perception. It is measurement.

The wealth numbers: The top 1% holds ~31% of total household wealth — roughly equal to the bottom 90% combined. The 905 U.S. billionaires hold $7.8 trillion. Worker productivity grew 80.9% from 1979-2024 while average hourly compensation grew only 29.4%. U.S. total household debt hit $18.6 trillion. Medical bills cause approximately 1 million bankruptcies per year. The racial wealth gap: White families hold 6× the wealth of Black families — virtually unchanged from 1992 to 2022.

Source: Raj Chetty / Opportunity Insights · Federal Reserve · EPI · Forbes 2025 · St. Louis Fed

Chapter 7 — Global Context

How America Compares on Surveillance and Democracy

The U.S. is not China — yet. But the gap between democratic surveillance and authoritarian surveillance has narrowed dramatically. And the legal safeguards that separate them are being dismantled in real time.

China

9/100 Internet Freedom

Social Credit System, Great Firewall, mass facial recognition, pervasive social surveillance. Worst-rated country globally for internet freedom.

United States

Capability Without Restraint

PRISM. Section 702. Data broker purchases without warrants. Palantir. DOGE data access. ICE AI surveillance. Freedom House: "legal safeguards have not always been strong enough." The capabilities now match authoritarian scope.

European Union

GDPR as Counter-Model

Fines up to €20M or 4% of global revenue. Rights to erasure, explicit consent, data portability. EU Court invalidated U.S.-EU Privacy Shield specifically over Section 702 concerns. The democratic standard exists — the U.S. hasn't adopted it.

Why We Do Business Here

What's Eroding

Rule of law, stable contracts, judicial independence, intellectual property protection, talent concentration. All of these depend on functioning democratic institutions. All are under documented stress in 2025-2026.

Source: Freedom House · EU GDPR · ECHR Schrems II · EFF · Brookings

Chapter 8 — The Trust Crisis

17% of Americans Trust the Federal Government

Down from 73% in 1958. The lowest recorded level since tracking began. Trust has not eroded abstractly — it has been broken by documented, specific failures and betrayals that people can name.

Political extremism is rising alongside distrust: Hate crimes hit 11,679 incidents in 2024 — second-highest ever. Anti-Jewish incidents reached 1,938 — the highest ever reported. FBI domestic terrorism investigations increased 357% from 2013 to 2021. The Trump administration in March 2025 cut the national database tracking domestic terrorism, gutted DHS prevention funding, and scaled back FBI domestic terrorism probes — while pardoning 1,575+ January 6 defendants. At least 33 pardoned insurrectionists have since been rearrested, 6 charged with child sex crimes.

Source: Pew Research · FBI · GAO · Washington Post fact-check database · CNN

Chapter 9

Protecting Yourself in a Surveillance State

You cannot opt out of a system that controls the infrastructure. But you can reduce your exposure, demand legal protections, and build institutions that aren't dependent on state goodwill.

Digital Privacy

Reduce Your Footprint

  • Use Signal for sensitive communications — end-to-end encrypted, FOSS
  • Use a VPN from a reputable provider with a no-logs policy
  • Opt out of data broker databases — search "data broker opt-out" for each major company
  • Use Firefox with uBlock Origin. DuckDuckGo for search.
  • Location data is the most sold data type — disable location tracking on all apps you don't need it for

Know Your Rights

Legal Protections

  • You have the right to remain silent with ICE — you do not have to answer questions without a lawyer
  • Know the Fourth Amendment Is Not For Sale Act — push your rep to pass it
  • Support EFF, ACLU, and state-level digital privacy coalitions
  • Demand your state pass a comprehensive data privacy law — 14 states have one; push yours to join

Build Power

Structural Solutions

  • Demand campaign finance reform — Citizens United has produced a 160-fold increase in billionaire spending
  • Support ranked-choice voting and open primary reform to break two-party capture
  • Run for local office or support someone who will — school boards, city councils, and county commissions still have real power
  • The 17% trust figure is a crisis AND an opportunity — when institutions fail, people build new ones
