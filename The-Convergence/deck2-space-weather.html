Chapter 1

Solar Cycle 25 Broke Every Prediction

Scientists predicted a weak cycle peaking around 115 sunspots. What actually happened was nearly double that — and it surprised even the researchers tracking it in real time.

Solar Cycle 25 began December 2019 and peaked in October 2024 with a smoothed sunspot number of 161 — 40% above the official prediction. Year 6 of SC25 (Dec 2024–Nov 2025) averaged 10% more sunspot activity than the equivalent period in Cycle 24 — and we're still in elevated activity through 2026.

Sources: NOAA SWPC · NASA Heliophysics · STCE Royal Observatory Belgium · helioforecast.space

Chapter 3 — The Research Is Real

Geomagnetic Storms Increase Heart Attack Risk

Peer-reviewed research across multiple countries and decades consistently finds that geomagnetic storm activity correlates with increased cardiovascular events. These are not fringe studies.

Feigin et al. 2014 (*Stroke*, American Heart Association) — 11,453 stroke patients, 16 million person-years, 5 countries: geomagnetic storms associated with a 19% increased stroke risk overall, rising to 52% for severe storms. Strongest effect in people under 65. Blood pressure rises 3–8 mmHg during geomagnetic storms are documented in multiple independent studies.

Sources: AHA Journals Stroke 2014 · PMC6739933 · PMC10589055 · Zilli Vieira et al. 2019 Environmental Health

Chapter 4 — The Research Is Real

Geomagnetic Storms and Mental Health Crises

Hospital admission records from multiple continents show the same pattern: when the sun gets active, mental health crises spike. This isn't anecdote. It's epidemiology.

Kay 1994 (British J. of Psychiatry): 36.2% increase in male hospital admissions for depressive illness the week following geomagnetic storms. Berk et al. South African study: correlation of r=0.69 (p<0.01) between geomagnetic activity and suicide rates. Dimitrova et al.: 87% of major geomagnetic storms coincided with surges in suicide attempt hospitalizations. Studies from Japan, Taiwan, Russia, Finland, Australia, and Canada have replicated these associations.

Sources: British J. Psychiatry 1994 · PMC7068248 · Dimitrova et al. 2010 Comptes Rendus Biologies

Chapter 7

Earth's Shield Is Weakening

Earth's magnetic field has weakened 9–10% over the past 200 years. The South Atlantic Anomaly — a growing weak spot in the field — has expanded by an area nearly half the size of continental Europe since 2014 alone.

The South Atlantic Anomaly has expanded to roughly 2 million square miles, now contains two separate minimum points, and its minimum field strength dropped from 24,000 to 22,000 nanoteslas between 1970–2020 (ESA Swarm). A weaker field means more cosmic radiation reaches the surface — particularly over South America and parts of Africa — and more vulnerability to solar storm effects. Mainstream science says this is normal variation, not an imminent reversal. The IYKYK community reads it differently. Both are worth knowing.

Sources: ESA Swarm · USGS · PNAS · ScienceDaily Feb 2026
